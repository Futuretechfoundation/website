Quantum Dots and Nanomaterials

Quantum dots are small semiconductor particles that exhibit quantum mechanical properties. Due to their size, quantum dots behave differently than bulk materials, and this behavior has important applications in electronics and photonics.

Quiz: Quantum Dots and Nanomaterials

Topological Materials and Quantum States

Topological materials, such as topological insulators, have surface states that are protected from disorder, making them useful in quantum computing and spintronics.

Quiz: Topological Materials

Metamaterials and Quantum Optics

Metamaterials are engineered materials with properties not found in nature. When combined with quantum principles, they can lead to unique quantum behaviors for light and matter interactions.

Quiz: Metamaterials

Quantum Transport Phenomena

Quantum transport phenomena such as tunneling and conductance quantization are fundamental to understanding how electrons move in materials at the quantum level, impacting electronics, energy conversion, and quantum devices.

Quiz: Quantum Transport Phenomena

2D Materials and Van der Waals Heterostructures

2D materials like graphene and transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) exhibit extraordinary electronic and mechanical properties. By stacking these materials into heterostructures, new quantum behaviors emerge that are useful for quantum devices.

Quiz: 2D Materials and Van der Waals Heterostructures

Metamaterials are only useful for optical purposes, not in quantum computing.

Strongly Correlated Electron Systems

Strongly correlated electron systems exhibit phenomena like high-temperature superconductivity and metal-insulator transitions. These behaviors arise due to the complex interactions between electrons, and are crucial for understanding quantum materials and developing new quantum devices.
