Course Syllabus

Welcome to Exploring Nanorobotics for Medicine and Engineering

Spring 2025

This comprehensive 8-week course explores cutting-edge nanorobotics principles, applications, and future frontiers. Through interactive modules, virtual labs, and expert guidance, you'll gain hands-on experience with the latest advancements in nanoscale robotics for medicine and engineering.

1. Medical Nanorobots for Targeted Drug Delivery

Nanorobots are transforming healthcare by delivering drugs directly to specific cells or tissues, reducing side effects and increasing treatment efficiency. Learn about stimuli-responsive nanobots that activate under certain conditions like pH, temperature, or magnetic fields.

Quiz
What is the primary benefit of targeted drug delivery by nanorobots?

A. Minimizes side effects B. Slows drug effectiveness

2. Nanorobots for Minimally Invasive Surgery

Learn how nanorobots perform minimally invasive surgeries by reaching delicate or hard-to-access areas of the body, such as repairing tissues or clearing blocked arteries. These advancements aim to reduce recovery time and complications.

Quiz
Which benefit is associated with nanorobots in surgery?

A. Reduces invasiveness B. Increases hospital stays

3. Personalized Medicine Nanorobotics

Personalized nanorobots adapt to an individual's unique genetic and physiological profile for precision treatment. They enable real-time health monitoring and deliver customized therapies.

Quiz
How do personalized nanorobots benefit healthcare?

A. Provide tailored treatments B. Offer generalized drugs

4. DNA Origami Nanorobotics

DNA origami is a technique that uses DNA strands to create precise nanostructures. These nanorobots have applications in drug delivery, nanoscale sensing, and molecular computation.

Quiz
What is the main principle of DNA origami nanorobotics?

A. DNA folding B. Protein folding

5. Nanorobotics Design and Simulation

Advanced design tools and simulations optimize nanorobots' performance, material selection, and control systems. AI-driven models improve efficiency and reliability in applications like healthcare and manufacturing.

Quiz
How does AI assist in nanorobotics design?

A. Predicts performance B. Randomly tests designs

6. Energy Harvesting and Self-Powered Nanorobots

Learn how self-powered nanorobots harvest energy from light, biofuels, or body fluids to sustain long-term operations without external power sources. These innovations enable nanobots to perform tasks autonomously in complex environments.

Quiz
Which energy source can be used by self-powered nanorobots?

A. Light B. Only electrical charge
