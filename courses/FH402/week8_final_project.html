FH402: Biotech: Next-Level Applications

Week 8 Final Project Guidelines

Project Overview

The Week 8 Final Project represents the culmination of your learning journey in FH402: Biotech: Next-Level Applications. This project challenges you to synthesize the knowledge, techniques, and ethical considerations explored throughout the course to develop an innovative biotechnology application proposal or prototype.

Your project should demonstrate both technical understanding and critical thinking about the societal implications of cutting-edge biotechnology. This is your opportunity to showcase your ability to envision and potentially develop biotechnology solutions for real-world challenges.

Project Requirements

Your final project must include the following components:

  • Problem Statement: Clearly identify a specific problem or opportunity that can be addressed through biotechnology applications.
  • Literature Review: Provide a concise review of current approaches and technologies relevant to your chosen problem.
  • Proposed Solution: Detail your innovative biotechnology approach, including methodology, anticipated outcomes, and technical specifications.
  • Feasibility Analysis: Assess the technological, economic, and practical viability of your proposal.
  • Ethical Considerations: Analyze potential ethical implications, risks, and mitigation strategies related to your proposal.
  • Implementation Timeline: Outline a realistic timeline for development, testing, and potential deployment.
  • Visual Elements: Include diagrams, models, or prototypes that illustrate key components of your proposal.

Project Types

You may choose one of the following project formats:

Research Proposal

Develop a comprehensive research proposal for a novel biotechnology application, including experimental design, methodologies, and expected outcomes.

Prototype Development

Design and develop a preliminary prototype or proof-of-concept for a biotechnology tool, process, or product that addresses your chosen problem.

Case Study Analysis

Conduct an in-depth analysis of an existing biotechnology application, evaluating its effectiveness, limitations, and proposing significant improvements or novel applications.

Policy Framework

Develop a detailed regulatory or policy framework for governing the implementation of a specific emerging biotechnology with significant societal implications.

Evaluation Criteria

Scientific/Technical Merit (25%)

Accuracy of scientific concepts, technical feasibility, and appropriate methodology.

Innovation & Creativity (20%)

Originality of approach, creative problem-solving, and innovative applications.

Analysis & Critical Thinking (20%)

Depth of analysis, consideration of alternatives, and logical reasoning.

Ethical Considerations (15%)

Thoughtful ethical analysis, risk assessment, and mitigation strategies.

Communication & Presentation (10%)

Clarity of writing, professional presentation, and effective use of visual elements.

Practical Relevance (10%)

Real-world applicability and potential impact of the proposed solution.
