FH402: Biotech: Next-Level Applications

Future Tech Academy - Advanced Biotechnology Series

This advanced course explores cutting-edge applications of biotechnology across multiple sectors, focusing on emerging technologies that are reshaping healthcare, agriculture, environmental science, and industrial processes. Students will gain both theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary to understand and contribute to the rapidly evolving field of biotechnology.

Upon successful completion of FH402: Biotech: Next-Level Applications, students will achieve the following learning outcomes:

Learning Outcomes

1. Knowledge & Understanding

  • Explain advanced principles of genetic engineering, including CRISPR-Cas9 technology and its applications in precision medicine
  • Describe current and emerging biosensor technologies and their implementation in healthcare monitoring systems
  • Analyze the principles behind synthetic biology and its potential to create novel biological systems
  • Identify key components of bioinformatics pipelines used in high-throughput genomic data analysis
  • Outline regulatory considerations and ethical frameworks governing advanced biotechnology applications

2. Technical & Practical Skills

  • Design experimental protocols for gene editing using current CRISPR-based methodologies
  • Perform computational analysis of genomic and proteomic datasets using industry-standard bioinformatics tools
  • Develop working models of biosensors for specific medical or environmental applications
  • Implement laboratory techniques for cell-free protein synthesis and directed evolution
  • Execute protocols for bioprocess optimization in biomanufacturing contexts

3. Critical Analysis & Problem Solving

  • Evaluate the scientific validity and commercial viability of emerging biotechnology platforms
  • Formulate solutions to complex bioengineering challenges using interdisciplinary approaches
  • Assess risks and benefits of novel biotechnologies across different application domains
  • Critique current limitations in biotechnology methods and propose innovative improvements
  • Apply systems thinking to analyze complex biological networks and their manipulation

4. Communication & Professional Development

  • Present technical biotechnology concepts effectively to both specialized and general audiences
  • Collaborate effectively in interdisciplinary teams addressing biotech challenges
  • Compose professional-grade research reports and technical documentation
  • Develop strategies for technology transfer and commercialization of biotech innovations
  • Navigate ethical dilemmas in biotechnology through structured ethical reasoning frameworks

Assessment Methods

Student achievement of these learning outcomes will be evaluated through:

Laboratory Practicals (30%)

Hands-on experiments implementing advanced biotech techniques with detailed lab reports assessing technical execution and scientific understanding.

Research Project (25%)

Individual or group-based investigation of a novel biotech application, culminating in a scientific paper and presentation to peers and faculty.

Case Study Analysis (15%)

Critical evaluation of real-world biotech implementations, their challenges, and their impact across different sectors.

Technical Portfolio (20%)

Compilation of practical work, data analysis outputs, and design proposals demonstrating technical competency in key areas.

Final Examination (10%)

Comprehensive assessment covering theoretical foundations and applications of advanced biotechnology concepts.
