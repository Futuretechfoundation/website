Textbooks & Reference Materials

Advanced Biotechnology: Principles and Applications (5th Edition)

Author: Dr. Mariana Chen & Dr. Robert Keller

Publisher: Oxford Scientific Press, 2024

This comprehensive textbook covers the latest advances in biotechnology applications, with special focus on CRISPR-Cas9 systems, synthetic biology, and biopharmaceutical production platforms.

Required Primary Textbook

Synthetic Biology: Engineering Life for Sustainable Solutions

Author: Dr. Jason Miller

Publisher: Cambridge University Press, 2023

This book explores how synthetic biology principles are being applied to address global challenges in health, environment, and industry.

Recommended Supplementary

Bioethics in the Age of Genetic Engineering

Author: Dr. Sarah Johnson & Dr. Michael Patel

Publisher: MIT Press, 2024

Explores the ethical implications of modern biotechnology applications, including gene editing, xenotransplantation, and genetic enhancement.

Recommended Ethics

Laboratory Resources

The following resources will be essential for your laboratory sessions during this course. Please familiarize yourself with these materials before attending lab sessions.

Lab Safety Guidelines

All students must review the Biosafety Level 2 (BSL-2) lab safety protocols prior to the first lab session. Download the complete lab safety manual from the course LMS.

Lab Techniques Documentation

Comprehensive guides for all techniques used in this course are available in the Lab Resources section of the course LMS.

Lab 1: CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Editing

Equipment: PCR thermocyclers, gel electrophoresis apparatus, microcentrifuges

Materials: gRNA design templates, Cas9 protein, competent cells

Pre-lab: Complete online CRISPR tutorial and quiz

Lab 2: Bioproduction Systems

Equipment: Bioreactors, spectrophotometers, HPLC system

Materials: Yeast strains, culture media, enzyme assay kits

Pre-lab: Review bioreactor operations manual

Lab 3: Biosensors Development

Equipment: Arduino kits, microplate readers, fluorescence microscopes

Materials: Biosensor components, reporter systems, sample analytes

Pre-lab: Complete Arduino programming basics tutorial

Course Schedule & Important Dates

Week 1-2: Foundations of Modern Biotechnology

Introduction to advanced biotech applications, CRISPR systems, and synthetic biology principles.

Assessment: Concept map assignment due end of Week 2

Week 3-4: Gene Editing Technologies

CRISPR-Cas systems, base editors, prime editors, and applications in medicine and agriculture.

Lab: CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Editing practical session

Week 5-6: Synthetic Biology Applications

Engineering biological systems, metabolic pathway design, and bioproduction platforms.

Lab: Bioproduction Systems practical session

Assessment: Midterm exam in Week 6

Week 7-8: Biosensors and Bioelectronics

Principles of biosensor design, cell-free systems, and applications in diagnostics and environmental monitoring.

Lab: Biosensors Development practical session

Week 9-10: Ethical and Regulatory Frameworks

Bioethics, biosafety considerations, global regulations, and responsible innovation.

Assessment: Ethics case study analysis due end of Week 10

Week 11-12: Future Horizons in Biotechnology

Emerging technologies, interdisciplinary applications, and career pathways.

Assessment: Final project presentations in Week 12

Final Exam: Comprehensive assessment in examination period
