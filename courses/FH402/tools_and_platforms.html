Frequently Asked Questions

Which platform should I learn first as a beginner in biotechnology?

For beginners, we recommend starting with bioinformatics platforms like Galaxy or R/Bioconductor as they require minimal lab equipment and provide a foundation in data analysis. From there, CRISPR systems offer accessible gene editing capabilities with widespread protocols available.

How much practical experience will I gain with these tools during the course?

FH402 includes 15 hours of hands-on laboratory work using key platforms introduced in the course. Additionally, students will complete a capstone project requiring application of multiple tools to solve a complex biotech challenge.

Are there opportunities to access expensive equipment beyond class time?

Yes, enrolled students can book additional lab time through our online reservation system. We also offer cloud-based access to certain computational platforms 24/7, and partnerships with industry labs for advanced equipment access.

How are these tools relevant to industry careers?

Each tool featured in this course was selected based on industry adoption rates and future growth projections. Our Industry Advisory Board reviews the curriculum annually to ensure alignment with current biotech sector needs and hiring requirements.
