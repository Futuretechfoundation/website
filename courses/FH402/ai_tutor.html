10:32 AM
A

Great question! Let me break down the key differences between these CRISPR systems:

CRISPR-Cas9:

  • Targets DNA specifically
  • Creates double-strand breaks
  • Requires NGG PAM sequence
  • The original and most widely used system

CRISPR-Cas12:

  • Also targets DNA but with different PAM requirements (often TTTV)
  • Creates staggered cuts rather than blunt cuts
  • Has collateral activity - once activated, can cleave nearby single-stranded DNA
  • Often smaller protein size, helpful for delivery

CRISPR-Cas13:

  • Targets RNA instead of DNA
  • No PAM requirement (instead has PFS - Protospacer Flanking Site)
  • Exhibits robust collateral RNA cleavage
  • Useful for RNA editing and diagnostics
